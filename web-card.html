San Antonio Mini Rice Terraces

Address: Aringay, La Union

The San Antonio Mini Rice Terraces were carved from mountain slope by the local residents. The rice paddies are fed by mountain stream that channels into an irrigation canal that runs downhill through the terraces.

The terraces were originally developed in the mid-1990s as part of a community development program that aimed to provide the local farmers with a more sustainable means of livelihood. Today, the terraces serve as a popular tourist attraction, drawing visitors from all over the world who come to marvel at their beauty and to learn about the unique farming practices that have been passed down from generation to generation.

Baluarte Watch Tower

Address: Gen. Peralta St, Brgy, Luna, La Union

The Baluarte Watch Tower, often referred to simply as "Baluarte," is an iconic historical structure located in the Philippines. Known for its significant historical and architectural importance, it serves as a testament to the country's rich past and its struggles for independence.

Baluarte Watch Tower holds a crucial place in the history of the Philippines. It is one of several watchtowers built along the country's coast during the Spanish colonial period, particularly in the 16th and 17th centuries. These watchtowers were essential for defending against pirates and other intruders who threatened coastal communities.

The Baluarte Watch Tower can be found in various coastal areas throughout the Philippines. These locations were strategically chosen to provide a wide view of the coastline and to serve as an early warning system against possible threats.
